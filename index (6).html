What this page does

Shows the structure before the volume

A strong portfolio is not a random gallery. It is a proof system. Each case study should explain the context, your role, the decisions you made, and the outcome that mattered.

Until those projects are live, the better move is to show the framework clearly rather than fill the page with fictional wins.

Suggested Case Study Structure
Client / Context
Who the work was for and what problem existed before the build.
Role
What you actually did: writing, design, strategy, development, research, or art direction.
Constraint
Budget, timeline, legacy system, brand limitations, or audience complexity.
Process
How you thought through the work and what decisions mattered most.
Result
Outcome, qualitative shift, or measurable impact.
